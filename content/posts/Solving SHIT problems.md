## Notes from the book: Solving S.H.I.T problems: Empowering teams to lead through crisis

## S.H.I.T

* Severe event
* Highly visible
* Information deficient
* Time-sensitive

### Leader

* Leader should create the value system that defines reality.
* Leader should push action.
* Leader should take responsibility.
* Leader should create open communication.
* Leader should create a secure environment so that errors can be admitted.

### Communication structures

* Create structures to promote 2 way frequent communication between the hierarchy.

### Cross-function technical experts

* Empower them.

### Learn by doing

* Do, experiment, fail fast to learn about the problem.

### Social construction of reality

* Leader should create a reality for the followers.

### Re-frame failure as an opportunity for knowledge

## Overall

Another book that could have been 30 pages long. But has some interesting ideas. I especially found this idea courages: The leader should socially construct reality.

First of all the book puts the leaders at the center as they should be. The book even calls them tsars.
The leader is the most important. So many make the mistake of ignoring the leader.
A leader leads. All else are secondary. The secondary parts like creating communication structures are important and vital but they are secondary.
Most make the mistake of taking the secondary part and show casing it as everything.

The book explicitly tells what must be done to lead:

* Socially construct reality
* Take action
* Take responsibility
* Create powerful teams of experts
* Create free 2-way communication.

It explicitly mentions that the leader should construct reality.
Not many dip their toes in post modernism and how value systems are created, and it is leader's job to create them.
